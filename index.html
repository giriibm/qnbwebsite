Nurses must be smart, adaptive, educated, and skilled in critical thinking. Nurse's responsibilities include coordinating with multiple specialists to ensure that their patients are adequately on the road to recovery. Through the different types of care, a nurse’s capabilities extend past their stereotypical personas; while many envision nurses donned in medical scrubs and running through a hospital, a nurse may come in many forms. Specifically, here are some of the things nurses do on a typical day

  • Conduct physical exams.
  • Take detailed health care histories
  • Listen to patients and analyze their physical and emotional needs.
  • Provide counseling and health care education to patients.

Frequently Asked Questions

Trusted local caregivers and nurses are available

  • Who needs home care?

    Home care can help the elderly age in place safely and comfortably. Those with special needs can also engage in-home care to support activities of daily living. Individuals who have acute, chronic, or terminal conditions, recovering from surgery, or need support with mobility can benefit from home care service.

  • What are the advantages of home nursing?

    Home nursing service allows care recipients to receive professional nursing care in familiar surroundings. This can reduce stress and help the care recipients feel more at ease, which aids in their recovery. By bringing nursing care to your doorstep, you can also save the hassle and cost of traveling and skip long queues at hospitals and clinics.

  • What services can home nursing provide?

    Besides supporting activities of daily living, our qualified and trained nurses can perform nursing procedures in the comfort of your home. Call our advisor for more information at +65 8171 2134

  • What services can a Caregivers provide?

    Our trained caregivers can assist seniors and adults with the following activities. This can involve: Personal hygiene, includes showering, toileting, diaper changing and dressing Mobility support, includes manual or assisted transfers to facilitate movement around the home and reduce fall-risk, Companionship to interact through activities and conversations to keep loneliness at bay staying active, includes home and outdoor exercises, Medication reminders for prompt medication consumption Meal preparation, purchase of takeaway or cooking meals suitable for the care recipient’s diet Medical escorts to doctor’s appointment and medication collection Check-in visits to ensure the safety of the care recipients who are home alone

  • Does QNB provide live-in caregivers?

    Our caregivers currently provide 24-hour care service but not live-in caregiving. For our 24-hour care service, two caregivers will be scheduled for the visits. Care will be split into 12-hour shifts to support you and your loved one round-the-clock each day

  • Who are the QNB Nurses and Caregivers?

    QNB nurses and caregivers are locals from all walks of life, who are passionate and dedicated to delivering quality care to families and care recipients islandwide in Singapore. All our caregivers have valid CPR certification and receive AIC-certified caregiver training. They are also required to go for a mandatory tuberculosis screening to ensure a clean bill of health. Before joining us as a Care Professional, QNB nursing services also conducts a face-to-face interview.
